Privacy Policy for EasyReport

Last updated: June 17, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains, in a clear and honest manner, how EasyReport handles your information. Please read it at your own pace: the summary is simple, and the rest of the document merely develops it in greater detail.

In a nutshell (an honest summary)

  • Everything stays on your device. EasyReport stores your reports, photos, annotations, and your company’s data solely in the local storage of your phone or tablet. There are no servers of ours, no cloud of our own, and no remote copy controlled by the developer.
  • The app does not transmit your content. EasyReport does not upload, send, copy, or remotely access your reports, photos, or company data. The developer does not receive that data.
  • No accounts and no sign-in. We do not ask you to create a username, an email address, or a password in order to use the app.
  • We do not track you. There is no advertising, no analytics, no advertising identifier (IDFA), and no third-party tracking SDKs.
  • You are in control of your data. You view, edit, and export it within the app, and you can delete your reports, photos, and company data from within the app itself. If you uninstall the app, all of the content it stores on your device is deleted.

If we later enable the paid subscription (the Pro feature), a minimal amount of data will indeed leave the device in order to process the charge. This is explained in detail in the section titled “Subscriptions (when available).” In the current version, this does not occur: no payment is processed and no data leaves the device.

However, as you will see throughout this document, the developer does not receive, does not store, and cannot access the data you generate with the app: the actual and ongoing control rests with you, on your device.

The current version of EasyReport is published for iOS (iPhone and iPad). An Android version is planned for the future. When that version becomes available, this policy will describe and justify the Android permissions, which will be kept to the minimum necessary for the same functions (taking and selecting photos, and saving images if you so choose). This policy will be updated with the corresponding date when that occurs.

3. What data the app handles and where it is stored

EasyReport is a professional tool that enables companies and field technicians to create reports with annotated photos and to export them to PDF bearing their own company’s branding.

To do its job, the app stores the following data exclusively in the local storage of your device (a database and files within the app’s private space, which on iOS is referred to as the sandbox):

  • Your company profile: company name, contact name, email, phone, footer and closing text, and the logos you upload.
  • Work structure: folders and reports (titles and dates).
  • Photos: the original photo and its annotated version.
  • Annotations: the freehand strokes and lines you draw over the photos (stored as drawing data).
  • “Pro” status: a local flag (yes/no) indicating whether you have the paid version activated. It is merely a value stored on your device; it is not an account or an identifier that is sent to anyone.

Important: all of this data lives solely on your device. There is no copy on the developer’s servers, because EasyReport has no servers and no cloud database. The developer does not collect this data within the legal meaning of the term (see Section 5).

Please note: if you have your device backup enabled (for example, iCloud or Google’s backup), your phone’s operating system may include the app’s data in that backup. That backup is managed by the system’s manufacturer (Apple or Google) under its own terms, not by the developer of EasyReport.

4. Device permissions: why we request them

In order to function, EasyReport needs certain permissions from your device. We request them only at the moment you need them (for example, the camera permission is requested when you are about to take a photo), not all of them when you open the app. The following are the permissions and their actual purpose in the iOS version:

  • Camera — “EasyReport uses the camera to take the photos for your reports.” It is used solely so that you can capture photos within the app.
  • Photo library access (read) — “EasyReport uses your photo library to add your company logo and photos to your reports.” It is used so that you can choose existing images (your logo or work photos) and add them to a report.
  • Save to camera roll (optional) — “EasyReport saves the photos you take to your camera roll, only if you choose to.” This permission is requested only if you choose to, after taking a photo, in order to save a copy to your gallery. It is entirely optional.

EasyReport does not use the microphone and does not record audio or video. If you decline any of the foregoing permissions, the app will continue to function reasonably; you simply will not be able to use the specific feature that depends on that permission.

5. Accessing your photos is NOT the same as collecting them

We want to be very clear on this point, because it is the key to how EasyReport works:

The fact that the app requests camera or photo library permission does NOT mean that the developer receives your photos.

EasyReport requests those permissions solely so that you can capture or select photos within the app. From that point onward:

  • The photos, annotations, company profile, and reports are stored exclusively in the local storage of your device.
  • EasyReport does not transmit, upload, copy, or remotely access any of this data.
  • The developer does not receive your information. There is no channel in the app that sends this data to the developer.

For this reason, in the App Store privacy label (Apple’s standardized summary), the current version of EasyReport is honestly declared as “Data Not Collected”: accessing a piece of data that remains solely on your device does not count as “collection” so long as that data does not leave the device toward the developer, and in the current version it does not.

6. The PDF and the sharing feature

When you export a report, the PDF is generated locally on your device. EasyReport does not send it anywhere on its own.

If you decide to share that PDF (by email, messaging, the cloud storage of your choice, etc.), you do so through your operating system’s own share sheet, and it is you who chooses the destination. From that moment, the file travels through the channel and to the recipient that you select, and it becomes subject to the privacy terms of that service (for example, your email provider). EasyReport does not control or take part in that transmission, and it cannot retrieve or delete a PDF that you have already shared.

7. No tracking, analytics, or advertising

EasyReport does not track you. Specifically:

  • It does not use usage analytics tools.
  • It does not display advertisements.
  • It does not access your device’s advertising identifier (IDFA).
  • It does not incorporate third-party tracking SDKs.
  • It does not track you across apps or across websites.

For this reason, EasyReport does not display the iOS “Allow Tracking” prompt (App Tracking Transparency): it does not need to, because there is no tracking to authorize.

8. Your rights and how to exercise them (control and deletion)

Because all of your data is stored solely on your device and you have direct access to it at all times, you exercise your rights natively, without having to request anything from us:

  • Access and rectification: you view and edit your information directly in the app (company profile, reports, photos, annotations).
  • Portability: you export your reports to PDF whenever you wish.
  • Erasure (right to be forgotten): you can delete your reports, photos, annotations, and your company’s data directly from the app. In addition, if you uninstall the app, all content it stores in the app’s private space on your device is deleted.

We are working to add a one-tap full-deletion option within the app; in the meantime, you can achieve the same result by deleting your items and/or uninstalling the app.

Please keep in mind the following limits, which are not within the app’s control:

  • If you have a system backup enabled (iCloud, Google), that backup may retain a version of your data until you manage it from your device settings or your Apple/Google account.
  • The photos you have saved to your camera roll and the PDFs you have already shared reside outside the app; if you wish to delete them, you must do so in your gallery or at the destination where you shared them.

In any event, the developer retains no copy of your report, photo, or company data, simply because it never receives that data. There is no “deletion request channel” for that data toward us, because there is nothing we can delete on our side: the control is entirely yours. (For the subscription data of the future Pro version, see Section 11.)

9. Users in the European Union and Spain (GDPR / LOPDGDD)

If you are located in the European Union or in Spain, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Spanish data protection legislation apply to you. We want to be transparent about how EasyReport fits within that framework:

  • Yes, the GDPR is applicable. We offer the app in Spanish to markets that include Spain, and the mere act of storing data—even if only on your device—is considered “processing.” We do not claim that the GDPR “does not apply.”
  • However, the processing on our part is minimal. By design, the developer does not receive, does not store, does not read, and cannot remotely delete your personal data from reports, photos, or your company. Effective control rests entirely with you.
  • Your GDPR rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection, and restriction) are satisfied directly through the features described in Section 8: you edit and export within the app, and you delete your items or uninstall the app.
  • Legal basis: for the data that remains on your device, the developer carries out no processing that would require its own legal basis, since it does not access that data. (When the paid subscription is activated, the legal basis for the associated processing will be the performance of the contract—managing your subscription—and the legitimate interest in preventing fraud and maintaining the service. See Section 11.)
  • Complaints: if you believe your rights are not being respected, you may contact us at hi@cuadria.xyz and, if you wish, lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority of your country (in Spain, the Spanish Data Protection Agency, www.aepd.es).

10. Users in California and the United States (CCPA / CPRA)

Regardless of whether California’s privacy legislation (CCPA/CPRA) applies to us based on size and business model, we want to make the following clear:

  • We do not sell your personal information.
  • We do not share your personal information for behavioral advertising or for any other purpose.
  • We do not use your data for cross-app or cross-site tracking.

We simply do not receive your report, photo, or company data: it is on your device, under your control. If you reside in California and wish to exercise any of your rights under the CCPA/CPRA, you may write to us at hi@cuadria.xyz.

11. Subscriptions (when available)

Current status: the version you are using is free and does not process any payment within the app. The app may display an informational screen about the future Pro version with indicative prices, but in this version nothing is charged, no purchase is completed, and no data leaves your device. The “Pro” status is solely a flag stored on your own device. This section describes how the Pro subscription will work when we activate it in a future version.

EasyReport offers the creation of up to 3 reports free of charge. To create reports without limit, you will be able to subscribe to the Pro version (for example, a monthly or annual fee; the exact, then-current prices will be displayed clearly on the subscription screen within the app at the time of purchase).

When you activate a paid subscription, two third parties will be involved and, for the first time, a minimal amount of data will leave your device in order to manage the charge:

Apple (payment processor)

The payment is processed by Apple through the App Store’s in-app purchase system. Apple manages your payment method and the transaction data in accordance with its own privacy policy. EasyReport never sees or receives the data of your card or payment method.

RevenueCat (subscription management)

To manage the status of your subscription, we will use RevenueCat, Inc. (a company headquartered in the United States), which will act as a data processor on our behalf. RevenueCat will receive only:

  • an anonymous user identifier generated by the app (a random code, not your name, nor your email, nor your phone number);
  • the status and history of your subscription (whether you are active, when it renews, etc.);
  • technical data about your device: model, operating system version, country, and app version.

What we will NEVER send to RevenueCat: your name, your email, your phone number, your company’s data, or the content of your reports or photos. All of that will remain exactly the same: solely on your device.

  • Purpose: to validate and manage your subscription and to prevent fraud.
  • Legal basis (GDPR): performance of the subscription contract and our legitimate interest in the proper functioning of the service.
  • International transfer: since RevenueCat is located in the United States, this transfer will be covered by the safeguards of RevenueCat’s data processing agreement (DPA), which includes the EU Standard Contractual Clauses.
  • Your rights over this data: to request access to or deletion of the subscription data associated with your anonymous identifier, write to us at hi@cuadria.xyz and we will handle it with RevenueCat.

When this feature is activated, we will update this policy and the App Store privacy label to accurately reflect this data, and we will note it in the “last updated” date.

15. Security

Your data is stored within the app’s private space on your device, protected by the security measures of the operating system itself (application isolation and, depending on your configuration, device encryption). Because we do not transmit or store your content on servers of our own, there is no risk of a breach of your data from the developer’s systems, because no such systems exist. No system is entirely infallible, so the physical protection of your device (passcode, biometrics) is an important complement that we recommend keeping active.
